Here's something a lot of ADT customers don't know: your monthly monitoring subscription may qualify you for a discount on your homeowner's insurance, and most people never bother to claim it. I didn't for the first several months I had my system. Once I did, I started saving about $120 a year — not life-changing, but it's real money that takes a chunk out of the monthly cost.

The process isn't complicated, but there are a few specific steps involved, and the amount you save varies depending on your insurer and what type of monitoring you have. This guide covers how the discount works, how much you can expect to save, and exactly how to claim it.

If you recently moved into a home and just activated ADT service, you can start this process right away. If you haven't activated service yet and you're still figuring out the first steps, [read the guide on what to do when you buy a house with ADT](/home-security-systems/bought-a-house-with-adt) first, then come back here.

## How the Discount Works

Insurance companies reduce premiums for homes with professionally monitored security systems because those homes are statistically less likely to result in a claim. A monitored alarm system that dispatches police quickly reduces both the likelihood of a successful break-in and the severity of loss if one does occur.

The discount is separate from any equipment or installation cost. It's purely a reduction in your annual insurance premium based on the monitoring service you're paying for.

One thing worth knowing upfront: the discount typically requires central station monitoring. That means professional monitoring through a company like ADT where a live agent receives alerts and dispatches emergency services. A DIY app where you monitor your own cameras and call 911 yourself does not qualify for this discount at most insurers.

## How Much Can You Save?

Most insurers offer between 5% and 20% off the annual premium for a home with professionally monitored security. The exact amount depends on three factors: your insurance company's policy, the type of monitoring coverage you have, and your home's base premium.

On a $1,500/year homeowner's insurance policy, a 10% discount saves $150 per year. That's $12.50 per month — a meaningful offset against ADT's $42.99 base monitoring cost.

The discount amount often scales with coverage. Intrusion monitoring alone (someone breaking in) typically gets you the lower end of the range. If your ADT plan also includes fire, smoke, and CO monitoring — which it does, if you have smoke and CO detectors connected to the system — most insurers will apply a larger discount. Insurers generally care more about fire risk than burglary risk, since fire claims are larger on average.

Insurance companies known to offer security system discounts include State Farm, Allstate, Nationwide, Liberty Mutual, and Travelers. The exact percentage varies by company and by state. Call your specific insurer and ask — and get the number in writing.

## How to Get the ADT Certificate of Alarm

To claim the discount, your insurer needs proof that you have active professional monitoring. ADT provides a document called a certificate of alarm (sometimes called a certificate of installation) that shows your address, the type of monitoring, the monitoring company name, and the service start date. This is the document you hand to your insurer.

<Admonition>
  Request the certificate as soon as your ADT service is activated. Some insurers will apply the discount retroactively to the start of your policy year, but only if you file within a certain window. Don't wait.
</Admonition>

There are two ways to get the certificate. The first is through the ADT app — log in, navigate to account settings, and look for a certificate or documentation option. The second is to call ADT directly at <a href={`tel:${companyData.phoneUnformatted}`}>{companyData.phone}</a> and request that they email or mail it to you. Either way, it usually takes 1 to 3 business days.

The certificate is typically a one-page document. Keep a copy for your records in addition to sending it to your insurer.

## How to File With Your Insurance Company

Once you have the certificate, the process is straightforward:

Call your insurance company and tell them you have active professional security monitoring with ADT. Ask them specifically what discount they offer for central station monitoring and whether they have a higher tier for fire and CO monitoring coverage.

Submit the certificate of alarm. Most insurers accept it by email, fax, or through an online customer portal. Some require it mailed in. Ask when you call.

Ask whether the discount requires annual renewal. Some insurers apply it once and it stays on the policy indefinitely as long as you have active monitoring. Others require you to submit a fresh certificate each year. If annual renewal is required, set a calendar reminder so you don't lose the discount without realizing it.

## The Fire and CO Monitoring Angle

This is worth a separate mention because a lot of ADT customers don't realize their smoke and CO detectors can be tied into the monitoring system. When they are, ADT's monitoring center receives an alert when a smoke or CO detector triggers, not just when a door sensor or motion detector goes off.

For insurance purposes, this matters. The discount for intrusion-only monitoring might be 5%. Add fire and CO monitoring and that can jump to 10% or more, depending on your insurer.

If your current ADT setup doesn't include smoke or CO detectors connected to the panel, ask ADT about adding them. The cost of a monitored smoke detector is relatively low compared to the ongoing insurance savings.

## My Personal Savings

I've been saving about $120 per year — roughly 8% on my homeowner's insurance — since I filed with my insurer after activating ADT. I have intrusion, fire, and CO monitoring, which put me in the higher discount tier with my carrier.

$120 per year doesn't fully offset a $48.99/month ADT bill. But it does take the effective cost down to about $39/month when you factor it in, which is competitive with other professionally monitored systems.

The process to claim it took about 20 minutes total: 10 minutes to request the certificate from ADT, 10 minutes on the phone with my insurer. That's a reasonable investment for money that comes back every year.
