I have reviewed both SimpliSafe and Cove individually. So in this article, I wanted to breakdown how they both compare one to another. So if you are on the fence about which one to get then this will be the guide for you.

The short version? Both are solid DIY security systems, and I don't think you'd be making a mistake going with either one. But they're not the same, and depending on what you care about, one is going to be a better fit than the other.

Here's everything you need to know.

## What Both of My Systems Came With

Both of my systems came with roughly the same equipment: a base station, keypad, door/window sensors, motion sensor, glass break sensor, and three cameras (indoor, outdoor, and doorbell).

The only extra on the SimpliSafe side was a smart door lock. Cove doesn't offer any sort of home automation (neither does SimpliSafe with the exception of the doorlock). It's security monitoring with cameras, and that's it.

Both are monitored 24/7 for police, fire, and medical. Both connect through Wi-Fi with cellular backup, so if your internet goes down or you lose power, monitoring stays active. There is no landline needed.

Both systems are Month-to-month, so you can cancel whenever without penality. That's a big pro for both and one of the main reasons people choose DIY systems over something like ADT or Vivint.

### SimpliSafe Cameras

SimpliSafe offers three cameras: outdoor, doorbell, and basic indoor. The standout feature is Active Guard. When coupled with their Active Guard monitoring plan ($49.99/mo), SimpliSafe's monitoring agents can access a live feed of your indoor cameras during an alarm to relay information to emergency services. They can even talk through the camera to assess the situation or scare off an intruder.

<figure>
  <Image
    src={simplisafeOutdoorCam}

  />

The Pro plan ($49.99/mo) uses Active Guard to monitor outdoor cameras between 8 PM and 6 AM. The Pro Plus plan ($79.99/mo) gets you 24/7 outdoor coverage. It's a premium, but it's a genuinely proactive security feature that you won't find with most other systems.

One issue I ran into: when viewing SimpliSafe's cameras live and someone was moving quickly (like walking around my pool), the video would stutter. The recorded playback was smooth, but live viewing had some lag.

The big downside is that SimpliSafe cameras are proprietary. If you cancel SimpliSafe service, your cameras stop working. You still have to pay SimpliSafe to get any use out of them.

### Cove Cameras

Cove partners with Eufy for their cameras. You get an indoor camera, an outdoor solar camera, and a doorbell camera. The quality was comparable to SimpliSafe, and I never had stuttering issues during live viewing. The video was smooth both live and in playback.

The biggest advantage with Cove's cameras: they're not proprietary. If you cancel Cove monitoring, you can download the Eufy app and keep using your cameras for free. That's huge if you're not sure about committing to monitoring long-term, or if you ever want to pause your subscription.

## Installation

SimpliSafe took me about two hours. Cove took about three. Between the two, SimpliSafe had a slightly easier setup process. They've been doing the DIY thing longer and have their process really refined. Cove was great too, but those camera connection issues slowed me down.

## Monitoring Plans And Pricing

### Cove Monitoring Plans

Cove keeps it simple with two plans:

| Feature                      | Cove Basic ($23.99/mo) | Cove Plus ($33/mo) |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------- | ------------------ |
| 24/7 monitoring              | Yes                    | Yes                |
| Cellular backup              | Yes                    | Yes                |
| Smartphone control           | Yes                    | Yes                |
| Camera support               | No                     | Yes                |
| Voice control (Alexa/Google) | No                     | Yes                |
| Equipment warranty           | 1 year                 | Lifetime           |
| $5/mo equipment credit       | No                     | Yes                |

If you want cameras with Cove, you have to go with the Plus plan at $33/mo. Out of that $33, Cove gives you a $5 credit each month that accumulates on your account. You can use it to buy additional equipment later. So your real out-of-pocket is closer to $28/mo.

One thing to know: Cove requires a monitoring plan. There's no self-monitoring option. If you cancel monitoring, the security system stops working. (Your Eufy cameras will still work through the Eufy app though.)

Cove also has a two-way voice feature built into the keypad panel. If your alarm goes off, the monitoring agents will try to reach you on your phone, but they can also talk to you through the panel. That can be useful if there's an actual break-in and you can't reach your phone.

### SimpliSafe Monitoring Plans

SimpliSafe has more options, which is both good and bad:

| Plan                  | Monthly cost | What you get                                                |
| --------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Free                  | $0           | Self-monitoring, camera live streaming, notifications       |
| Camera Recording Only | $9.99        | Cloud storage for 10 cameras, smart home integrations       |
| Standard              | $21.99       | 24/7 professional monitoring, cellular connection           |
| Core                  | $31.99       | Active Guard (indoor), video-verified alarms, cloud storage |
| Pro                   | $49.99       | Active Guard (outdoor, 8PM-6AM)                             |
| Pro Plus              | $79.99       | Active Guard (outdoor, 24/7)                                |

The free plan is a real option if you're on a tight budget. You get live streaming and notifications on your phone, so you can still monitor things yourself. You just won't have professional monitoring dispatching police or fire for you.

The Core plan at $31.99 is the sweet spot for most people. It's where you get Active Guard on your indoor cameras, video-verified alarms (the monitoring center gets a video clip when an alarm triggers), and cloud storage.

If you want agents actively watching your outdoor cameras, that's the Pro or Pro Plus tier. It's a premium, but it's a genuinely different approach to home security. Most systems are reactive. Something trips the alarm, then help gets dispatched. Active Guard is proactive. An agent can spot someone suspicious on your property and intervene before a break-in even happens.

## How They Handle Emergencies

Both systems dispatch police, fire, and medical 24/7 through their monitoring centers.

Cove uses RapidSOS technology, which routes emergency calls with additional data so the right service responds faster. If your smoke alarm goes off, the fire department is contacted. If someone breaks in, it goes to the police. Cove also has three monitoring centers across the U.S. for redundancy, plus that two-way voice through the panel.

SimpliSafe's professional monitoring is run by Rapid Response using wireless and cellular platforms. If you're on the Core plan or higher, video-verified alarms send a clip to the monitoring center when your system triggers, which can speed up response times. And of course, Active Guard takes it further by letting agents assess the situation through your cameras in real time.

If you're on SimpliSafe's free plan, you're on your own for contacting authorities.

## Smart Home And Home Automation

I'll keep this short because there's not much to say. Neither system is a smart home hub. If you want to control lights, thermostats, and locks all from one app, look at Vivint or ADT.

Cove has zero home automation. SimpliSafe has one proprietary smart door lock you can sync to the system. That's it. Both work with Alexa and Google Assistant for voice commands, but only for basic security functions like arming and disarming.

## So Which One Should You Get?

After living with both, here's how I see it.

### Get Cove If You Want The Best Value

<ProsCons company="cove" />

Cove is the system I'd recommend for most people who just want solid security without overpaying. The upfront cost is about half the price of SimpliSafe (especially with their 70% off deals). The cameras aren't proprietary, so you can keep using them if you ever cancel. The touchscreen keypad is better. And the monthly cost is basically the same as SimpliSafe's comparable plan, with the added bonus of that $5/mo equipment credit.

The monitoring is reliable, the equipment gets the job done, and you're not locked into anything. If you want core home security with cameras at the lowest possible price, <a href={coveCompanyData.website}>check out Cove</a>.

### Get SimpliSafe If You Want Active Guard

<ProsCons company="simplisafe" />

If Active Guard is something you want, SimpliSafe is the only game in town. Having monitoring agents actually watch your camera feeds and intervene before a break-in happens is a different level of security. It's not cheap ($49-$80/mo for outdoor coverage), but nothing else in the DIY space offers anything like it.

SimpliSafe also wins if you want a free self-monitoring option, a bigger equipment selection, or a more polished app. The pre-built packages make it easier to buy a system without decision fatigue.

### The Bottom Line

For my money, I lean towards Cove. Lower upfront cost, non-proprietary cameras, better keypad, and the same monthly price for core monitoring. But if Active Guard is a must-have or you want the free monitoring tier, SimpliSafe is the move.

Either way, you're getting a capable DIY system with no contracts and professional monitoring. You're not making a bad decision with either one.
