ADT has been around for over 100 years, which means there are a lot of ADT systems out there that are still technically working but running on technology that's well past its prime. Push-button keypads with no screen. Landline-based monitoring. Cameras that haven't been manufactured in years. If that sounds familiar, this guide is for you.

The good news is that ADT upgrades existing customer systems, and the process is more straightforward than most people expect. Before going further, if you've been using your current system for a while and aren't sure whether it's underperforming, run through the [ADT maintenance checklist for homeowners](/home-security-systems/adt-maintenance-checklist) first — it'll tell you whether you're dealing with a maintenance issue or a genuine equipment limitation.

If the problem is the equipment itself, read on.

## Signs Your ADT Equipment Is Outdated

The clearest sign is the keypad. If your ADT panel has physical buttons and no touchscreen, you're using equipment that predates ADT's current lineup by several years. Push-button keypads can still arm and disarm the system, but they don't support the ADT app, can't display camera feeds, and don't work with smart home automations.

<figure>
  <Image src={adtPanelHome} alt="ADT Command Panel touchscreen home screen" />
  <figcaption>The current ADT Command Panel has a touchscreen. If yours doesn't, the panel is a good place to start with upgrades.</figcaption>
</figure>

Other signs:

Your system uses a landline. If your ADT monitoring connects through a phone line, that's the oldest type of connection ADT used and it's being phased out across the industry. Landline-based monitoring is less reliable than cellular and easier to defeat — cutting the phone line outside the house can knock out a landline-based system. Cellular monitoring doesn't have that vulnerability.

You can't access the ADT app. The app requires the Command Panel and a cellular connection. If you're missing either, you have no remote access to your system.

Your cameras are old ADT-branded cameras, not Nest. ADT's current lineup uses Google Nest cameras exclusively. If your cameras were installed before the Nest partnership, they're running on older hardware and software that ADT no longer actively develops.

Your sensors are losing connection frequently. Older wireless sensors used 433MHz protocols. Newer sensors use more reliable frequencies. Frequent zone drops or false alerts can indicate aging sensor hardware, though it can also just be a battery issue — the maintenance checklist will help you tell the difference.

## What the Modern ADT Setup Looks Like

The current professionally installed ADT system has four main components worth knowing about:

The Command Panel is a touchscreen device that sits on a table or shelf near your front door. It's the hub for arming and disarming, checking sensor status, and managing settings. It's also what enables the ADT app and smart home integrations.

<figure>
  <Image src={adtPanelZones} alt="ADT Command Panel showing security zones on screen" />
  <figcaption>The zones screen on the Command Panel shows every sensor in the home and its status. This is a lot more useful than a list of blinking lights on a push-button panel.</figcaption>
</figure>

The Nest cameras are HD cameras with night vision, two-way audio, and AI-powered motion alerts. The doorbell camera handles the front door. An outdoor camera covers the perimeter. Additional Nest cameras can be added without increasing the monthly cost.

<figure>
  <Image src={adtNestLineup} alt="ADT and Nest equipment lineup" />
  <figcaption>ADT's current equipment lineup. The Nest cameras replaced ADT's older proprietary cameras several years ago.</figcaption>
</figure>

Z-Wave smart home devices connect to the Command Panel through the ADT app. Smart locks, thermostats, light switches, and garage door controllers all work through the same interface. ADT is also compatible with Nest thermostats.

Cellular monitoring replaces any landline connection. The system communicates with ADT's monitoring centers via cellular signal, not a phone line. Backup battery keeps the panel and cellular connection running during power outages.

## How to Request an Upgrade

Call ADT at <a href={`tel:${companyData.phoneUnformatted}`}>{companyData.phone}</a> and ask for a system assessment. This is a scheduled technician visit where ADT sends someone to your home to evaluate what you currently have, identify what's outdated, and recommend specific upgrades. There's no charge for the assessment itself.

During the call, be specific about what you're experiencing. "My panel has no screen and I can't access the app" is more useful than "I think my system is old." Tell them the model number if you can find it — it's usually on the back of the panel. That lets them prepare before the visit.

## What Upgrades Cost

The cost varies significantly depending on what you're replacing. A rough breakdown:

Upgrading just the panel to a Command Panel runs roughly $150 to $300 for the equipment, depending on any promotions running at the time. If you're an existing customer and your contract is in good standing, ADT sometimes offers panel upgrades at a reduced cost — worth asking about specifically.

Adding cameras depends on which cameras and how many. ADT frequently runs promotions for existing customers. The current offer for new customers is a free Nest Doorbell and Nest Camera, which is worth checking on even as an existing customer since promotions do extend to upgrades.

Adding sensors is relatively low-cost on a per-unit basis. Door and window sensors, motion sensors, and glass break sensors are typically $20 to $50 each when added outside of a promotional package.

Upgrading from landline to cellular is often lower cost than people expect, because it's primarily a software and hardware change at the panel level rather than a full system replacement. Call ADT and ask — this upgrade is worth prioritizing if it's the one situation that applies to you.

## Adding Nest Cameras Yourself

Once ADT has professionally installed your first set of Nest cameras, you can add more on your own. Buy a Nest camera from any retailer — Amazon, Best Buy, Walmart, wherever — and add it to the Google Home app. It'll show up in your ADT system without any additional monthly cost.

## Smart Home Upgrades Worth Considering

If you're upgrading the panel and gaining access to the ADT app for the first time, this is a natural point to think about Z-Wave smart home devices. They don't all need to be installed at once — you can add them one at a time as you decide what's useful.

<figure>
  <Image src={adtSmartLock} alt="ADT smart lock controlled from the app" />
  <figcaption>A Z-Wave smart lock works with the ADT app once you have the Command Panel. You can lock and unlock remotely, set temporary codes, and tie it into system automations.</figcaption>
</figure>

A smart lock on the front door is the most practical starting point for most people. You can lock and unlock remotely, set up temporary codes for contractors or family, and create an automation that locks the door automatically when you arm the system.

A Nest thermostat is the other one I'd prioritize. It's compatible with ADT's system and also adds Google's own smart scheduling features on top of that.

Lights and garage door controllers are nice to have, but less essential. Add them when it makes sense rather than all at once.

## What to Ask ADT When You Call

A few specific questions worth having ready:

Ask whether the upgrade will change your contract terms. Get a clear answer before committing.

Ask about current promotions for existing customers. Upgrade deals don't always match new-customer deals, but they exist.

Ask whether the technician can assess the full home during the visit — not just replace the panel, but also check sensor placement, battery status, and cellular signal strength. A thorough visit is more useful than a quick swap.

Ask specifically about the landline situation if your current system uses one. This is the upgrade I'd push hardest for. Cellular monitoring is more reliable, and landline-based panels are being phased out.
