When I first called ADT to set up my system, I expected a complicated pricing menu. What I got was actually pretty simple: two main plans for the professionally installed system. That's it. The hard part isn't understanding the plans — it's figuring out which one makes sense for your situation.

This guide is for new homeowners who are either just signing up with ADT or still comparing their options. If you moved into a home with ADT equipment already installed and you're trying to figure out whether to activate it, I'd start with [what to do when you buy a house with ADT](/home-security-systems/bought-a-house-with-adt) before reading this.

For everyone else, here's everything you need to know about ADT monitoring costs and what each plan actually includes.

## ADT Monitoring Plans at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly cost | Cameras included | Nest Aware | Smart home |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard monitoring | $42.99 | No | No | Yes |
| With cameras | $48.99 | Yes | Yes (30 days) | Yes |

That's the short version. Both plans include 24/7 professional monitoring, the ADT app, cellular backup, and full smart home automation. The camera plan adds Nest camera support and the Nest Aware subscription, which normally costs $10/month on its own through Google.

## What the Standard Plan Includes

At $42.99 per month, you get everything that makes ADT worth having: around-the-clock monitoring by a live agent at one of ADT's six monitoring centers, 24/7 dispatch for police, fire, and medical emergencies, and full access to the ADT app on your phone.

  <Image src={adtApp} alt="ADT app showing armed notification on a phone" />

The monitoring runs on cellular — there's no landline needed, and if your power or internet goes out, the system stays connected. ADT's six monitoring centers are spread across the country so that if one goes down, coverage doesn't drop.

The standard plan also includes the smart home features: you can control Z-Wave locks, lights, thermostats, and garage doors through the app, and set up automation rules like "lock the door every time I arm the system." If you have Nest devices (other than cameras), those work here too.

What you don't get is camera support. You can have Nest cameras in your home, but they won't be tied into your ADT monitoring or managed through the ADT system on this plan.

## What the Camera Plan Includes

For $48.99 per month, you get everything in the standard plan plus Nest camera integration and the Nest Aware subscription. Nest Aware includes 30 days of cloud video storage for all your cameras, smart alerts with AI-powered descriptions (instead of generic "motion detected" notifications), and event history you can scrub through in the Google Home app.

Here's the part I think makes the camera plan an easy call: Nest Aware costs $10/month on its own if you buy it directly from Google. ADT's camera plan is only $6 more than the standard plan. So you're effectively getting Nest Aware at a $4 discount, and you're getting full ADT integration on top of that.

<Admonition>
  The monthly price stays the same no matter how many Nest cameras you add. My plan covers a doorbell camera and an outdoor camera for $48.99/month. You could add four more cameras and the price wouldn't change.
</Admonition>

After ADT professionally installs your initial cameras, you can buy additional Nest cameras from any retailer — Amazon, Walmart, wherever — and add them yourself through the Google Home app. They'll show up in your ADT system automatically. That's a genuinely useful feature if you want to expand coverage over time without calling a technician.

## How the Contracts Work

ADT's contract structure changed a few years ago. Here's how it works now:

If you pay for equipment upfront, you're on a 12-month agreement. That's the minimum commitment, and it's what I chose. Twelve months is still a contract, but it's a lot easier to stomach than the old three-year deals ADT used to require.

If you finance the equipment — meaning the cost gets rolled into your monthly bill — the contract extends to 36 months or 60 months depending on the financing plan. The monthly cost goes up, but you pay nothing upfront for equipment.

<Admonition>
  Canceling mid-contract triggers an early termination fee, typically 75% of the remaining monthly payments. If you're not sure you'll stay in your home for a year, pay for equipment upfront and keep your commitment to the 12-month minimum.
</Admonition>

One thing worth knowing: if you already have equipment in your home from a previous owner, you may be able to skip the equipment cost entirely and just pay for monitoring. Call ADT to discuss — existing equipment can sometimes be reactivated at a lower upfront cost.

## What to Ask About When You Call

ADT's pricing isn't always posted online in full detail. When you call, you'll talk to a sales rep who can walk through equipment options and give you a real quote for your home. A few things worth asking about specifically:

First, ask about current equipment promotions. ADT frequently runs offers for new customers. The current promo is a free Nest Doorbell and Nest Camera with new service — that's $200+ in equipment at no additional charge. It's the kind of thing that doesn't always get mentioned upfront unless you ask.

Second, ask about the system assessment. Before committing, you can have a technician come out and assess your home to recommend the right number of sensors and camera placement. It's free, and it means you're not guessing how many door sensors you need or where to put the motion detector.

Third, if you have existing equipment in the home from a previous ADT customer, mention that when you call. It may change the equipment cost and potentially the contract terms.

## My Honest Take on the Two Plans

I pay $48.99/month for the camera plan, and I don't think about the $6 difference. The Nest Aware subscription alone justifies it. I look back at camera footage a few times a month — mostly after I get an alert I'm not sure about — and 30 days of cloud storage means I can always find what I'm looking for.

The standard plan makes sense if you genuinely don't want cameras and have no plans to add them. Some people just want the alarm and the monitoring dispatch — that's a legitimate choice, and $42.99/month for that is competitive with other professionally installed systems.

But if there's any chance you'll add cameras later, go with the camera plan from the start. Upgrading partway through a contract adds friction, and you'll already be paying for Nest Aware at that point anyway.
