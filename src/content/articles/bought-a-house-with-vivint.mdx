Congratulations on your new home purchase! If your house came equipped with a Vivint smart security system, you're already on the right path towards protecting your new home. Navigating the next steps with an existing Vivint setup can feel overwhelming, but this guide is here to make activating the system as easy as possible.

I'll cover everything new homeowners need to know about the existing system including pricing, ownership, features, and finally how to get the system up and running.

## The Previous Home Owners Left Behind the Vivint Panel and Equipment

If it's any other panel, then Vivint won't be able to activate the system and you'll be required to buy a new system upfront.

You may be wondering "Is this equipment mine to keep? Or does Vivint need this back?" The good news is that it's yours to keep! So what you do next is up to you. This means you can either call Vivint to activate the system or toss it in the trash. I'd recommend keeping it since the equipment cost can be between $600–$2,000. That is what everyone ordering the system brand new must pay.

If you're shocked by that price tag, just know that Vivint is like the Apple of alarm systems. It has both great hardware and software, but that does come with a price. Fortunately, you won't have to pay that.

Lastly, since you are only activating the system, it's month-to-month service. No contract. All you would pay is just your first month to get it up and running. It's one of the better deals in home security.

## How do I Activate the Vivint System and Get a Pin Code?

Here is what you'll need to do in order to get your account set up and get a pin code:

1. Call Vivint at 833-606-2573 and press 1 to speak to sales (in order to use the system or get a pin code you must be a customer)
2. Give the sales rep your address for them to search for the old account
3. Once they find the old account, they can tell you what is installed and discuss the features of the system with you
4. To activate the service, you'll need to pay your first month of service — usually between $29.99–$49.99 per month (no contract) — with the rep over the phone
5. The sales rep will then schedule another phone call with the panel activation team to remotely activate the service, set up your account info, and set up a pin code to use the system

Once the system is activated, you'll have access to the 24/7 monitoring service, access to the app on your phone, as well as any other features with the system such as cameras, door locks, etc.

The good news is that when you activate an existing system, you won't have a contract. You'll also save a good amount of money since you don't have to buy the equipment too.

Due to security reasons, Vivint will not give out the old pin code from the previous account owner. If you don't want to use the system but still need the pin code, your best bet will be to reach out to the previous owners. Otherwise, you'll need to call Vivint or visit their website to activate the service and get the pin. Since it is month to month, you can always cancel the service after getting your pin if you really don't see yourself using it.

## How Much is the Monthly Price to Just Activate It?

Vivint home security has three different pricing plans:

| Plan                              | Pricing   |
| --------------------------------- | --------- |
| Monitoring w/ App                 | $29.99/mo |
| Monitoring + Smart Home           | $39.99/mo |
| Monitoring + Smart Home + Cameras | $49.99/mo |

The $29.99 plan covers basic monitoring to police, fire, and medical. This will also cover the app on your phone. For $39.99 per month, you get everything in the $29.99 plan, plus the ability to control any smart home items left behind from the app — meaning if they have an automatic door lock or thermostat, you can now control that from your phone. Finally, for $49.99 you can control everything including the cameras, giving you access to monitor them from your smartphone wherever you are.

If you'd like to upgrade to a higher tier later, that's easy — just call Vivint support at any time.

Their service is month to month. Most companies require a 3 year contract or make you pay hundreds of dollars upfront. If you have Vivint in your home already, you can cancel the service anytime without penalty. This makes it one of the best deals in the security industry, assuming all the equipment is already in there.

## What Features are Included in the System?

Vivint comes standard with 24/7 monitoring to police, fire, and medical regardless of what plan you choose. Every system also comes with the panel and Vivint sensors. Below is a quick breakdown of the different types of sensors:

- Door/window sensors
- Motion sensors
- Glass break sensors
- Garage door sensors
- Flood sensors
- Smoke detectors

In addition to the sensors, there are three different cameras that they offer: the outdoor camera, indoor camera, and doorbell camera. These cameras are professionally installed by a technician. If you want to add more cameras, you can call 833-606-2573.

Lastly, there are the smart home devices:

- Thermostat
- Automatic door lock
- Light switches
- Smart plugs

All of those devices connect to the system and can be controlled by the app. Their software also gives you the ability to set up different automations throughout the house. For example, you can set up a bedtime automation where every night at 10PM the system locks the door, arms the alarm system, and turns off the lights.

## Can I Use Vivint Equipment without Monitoring?

Yes, you can use Vivint equipment without monitoring. However, you cannot use the app or account view on your phone and there will be no monitoring. You can still arm and disarm the alarm, and have it set up to automate things such as a door lock or thermostat whenever you leave.

If someone were to try and break in while the system is armed, it will sound the siren but will not call the police.

You will need the 4-digit pin code to arm and disarm the alarm. You can call the previous home owners to ask them what it was. If you call Vivint they will not give that info away for obvious reasons. So if you can't get in contact with the previous home owners, you can call Vivint to activate the system for one month, get a new pin code, and then cancel the service after that. Their monitoring service is month to month, so they will have to give you a pin code once it's activated — and the pin code will still work after you cancel.

## Is the Equipment Mine to Keep?

If you bought the house and the equipment is still in there, then it is yours to keep. If the previous customer failed to move the equipment with them and still owes money on it, they will have to settle it with Vivint. Vivint does not go back for its equipment if a person defaults on their payment.

If the customer had notified Vivint before moving, they would have asked them to move the panel to their new house along with some of the smart home items. Normally they give them new Vivint sensors at the new place.

## Can I Add on Extra Equipment to My House Like Cameras?

Yes, you can add on any extra equipment that Vivint offers after the service is activated. They will have you pay upfront for the item and then send a tech out for professional installation. If you want over $600 worth of equipment, you can finance that amount at 0% APR if you qualify. They will then roll the monthly bill into your payment, kind of like a cell phone plan.

## I Accidentally Triggered the Alarm, How Do I Turn it Off?

If you accidentally set the alarm off and you don't have the pin code, there isn't too much you can do. The only option is to remove the panel from the wall and then remove the back battery. This will get the panel to turn off and shut the siren down. If the system is not being monitored, you don't have to worry about the police coming. You can find a guide to removing the panel and taking out the battery on Vivint's support site.

You can sometimes try the codes 1234, 2468, or 2580 to turn the system off, because a lot of times that is what people leave their code as. It is not a guarantee that it will work though.

If you want to activate the service and get support, you can contact Vivint at 833-606-2573 or visit their website.

## Vivint vs. Other Home Security Systems

Vivint is top of the line in terms of both hardware and software. It comes with all the bells and whistles that you would hope for in a system and simply works out of the box exactly like you would expect.

However, if you don't want Vivint, I'd recommend either Cove or ADT. Cove is a newer company with affordable equipment and monitoring services. Their price point for both equipment and monthly pricing is a great value — the equipment is really affordable but still good quality, and the same goes for their monitoring. The only catch is that it's all DIY, so there is no professional installation. Pricing for the equipment is around $300 and their monthly cost is around $30 per month.

ADT is Vivint's top competitor. They offer similar services and equipment quality, with a monthly starting at around $49.99 per month if you pay for the equipment upfront.
