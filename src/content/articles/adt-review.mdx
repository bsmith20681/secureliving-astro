I've had the ADT home security system installed in my home for a while now, and I've been testing things out. This is my full, honest review of the system. I paid for all the equipment out of my own pocket, so everything here is based on real, everyday use. No sponsorship, no freebies. I'll cover the equipment, cameras, monitoring, smart home features, pricing, and all the things I think you should know before signing up.

If you're reading ADT reviews trying to figure out if this system is actually worth the money, I think this will help you make that call.

## Quick look at the pros and cons

Before I get into all the details, here's a quick snapshot of where ADT lands for me after living with it day to day.

## What is ADT?

ADT is one of the oldest and most recognizable names in home security. The company has been around for over 100 years (they started as American District Telegraph, if you were wondering what ADT stands for). They offer professionally installed home security systems with 24/7 monitoring, and they've recently partnered with Google to use Nest cameras and smart home products across their lineup.

They also now offer a DIY self-setup option, but this review covers the professionally installed system. That's the version with the touchscreen command panel, and in my opinion, it's the better experience.

What sets ADT apart from a lot of the DIY competitors is the monitoring infrastructure. They have six monitoring stations spread across the country, so if one goes down, your system is still covered. For a company that's been doing this as long as they have, they clearly know how to keep things running.

The command panel is the centerpiece of the ADT system. It's a touchscreen that sits on a table or shelf (mine is near the front door), and you use it to arm and disarm the system, change settings, add user PINs, and manage your sensors.

I really like the touchscreen. It's way more intuitive than the old-school keypads or the puck-style base that comes with the DIY version. You can tap through menus, check the status of all your zones, and adjust things without pulling out your phone. If you've used a tablet, you'll feel right at home with this panel.

One thing worth knowing: if you go the DIY route with ADT, you don't get this panel. You get the ADT Base instead, which is a smaller cylindrical hub with a touch keypad on top but no screen. It works fine for arming and disarming, but you lose the ability to browse settings, view camera feeds, or manage the system visually. That was a big reason I went with the pro install.

## ADT's security monitoring

The system is monitored 24/7 for police, fire, and medical emergencies. It all runs through a cellular connection, so there's no need for a landline. If your Wi-Fi goes out or even if you lose power, the system stays connected and keeps working. That was one of the first things that sold me on it.

ADT has six monitoring stations across the country. That's more than most competitors. Vivint has two. SimpliSafe outsources their monitoring to a third party. Having that many stations means there's built-in redundancy. If one center goes offline for any reason, the others pick up the slack. For something as important as your home security, that kind of reliability matters.

I know ADT gets some complaints online. But when you look at the sheer number of customers they have compared to the number of complaints, they're clearly doing something right. They wouldn't still be in business after 100+ years if the monitoring didn't work.

### ADT monitoring plans

ADT offers two main tiers for the professionally installed system:

- **Standard plan ($42.99/month):** This covers 24/7 monitoring, the ADT mobile app, and access to smart home controls. No camera support on this tier.
- **Camera and smart home plan ($48.99/month):** This is the plan I'm on. It includes everything in the standard plan plus support for Nest cameras, 30 days of cloud video storage (Nest Aware is included), and the full smart home automation features.

If you're getting cameras with your system, you'll want the $48.99 plan. The Nest Aware subscription alone is $10/month if you buy it separately from Google, so having it baked into the ADT price is a nice bonus.

## Sensors and security equipment

  <Image src={adtDoorSensor} alt="ADT door sensor installed on a door frame" />

    and connect to the panel automatically.

My system came with door/window sensors, glass break sensors, and a motion detector. The sensors connect wirelessly to the command panel. If you have an older hardwired system in your home, ADT can actually take over those existing sensors too, which saves you money since you don't have to buy all new ones.

The door and window sensors are small and discreet. They stick on with adhesive and do exactly what you'd expect. Open a door or window while the system is armed and you'll get an alert. The glass break sensor is an acoustic sensor that listens for the sound of breaking glass. The technician placed mine facing my sliding glass doors, which is where it works best.

The motion sensor uses passive infrared to detect movement. If you have pets, you'll want to adjust the sensitivity. ADT says theirs can ignore pets up to 85 pounds, which is better than most competitors I've tested. I set mine to a lower sensitivity and haven't had issues with false alarms.

One thing I appreciate about the professional install is that the technician knows where to place everything for the best coverage. They assessed my home, figured out the vulnerable points, and placed the sensors accordingly. If I had done it myself, I probably would have missed a few spots.

For a while, ADT was using their own proprietary cameras. There was even a period where they partnered with Ring. But now, whether you go with the pro install or the DIY system, you're getting Google Nest cameras. And honestly, I'm really happy with them.

My setup includes the Nest Doorbell camera and a Nest outdoor camera. Both are high definition with night vision and built-in motion detection. The Nest Aware subscription is included with ADT's camera plan, which gives you 30 days of cloud video storage. You can save recordings to your phone, share clips with family members, and review footage anytime. If you want 24/7 continuous recording, that's available for an extra $10/month.

### Adding more cameras later

This is one of the best parts about ADT using Nest cameras. After your initial setup, you can buy additional Nest cameras from anywhere (Walmart, Amazon, Facebook Marketplace, wherever) and add them to your system yourself. Your monthly price stays the same regardless of how many cameras you have. Two cameras, four cameras, eight cameras. Same bill. That's a really nice perk.

### A few camera cons to know about

No camera is perfect, and these have a few drawbacks worth mentioning.

There's no native Power over Ethernet (PoE) option. The cameras rely on Wi-Fi, so if your internet connection isn't great, the video quality can suffer. A wired ethernet connection would make things more stable, but that's not available with these models.

The cameras can technically record without Wi-Fi, but only up to one hour of footage. There are other cameras on the market that can record locally without any Wi-Fi at all, so one hour feels a bit limited.

And then there's the app situation, which I'll get into next.

My biggest gripe with the app situation is that you currently need two apps. The ADT app handles the alarm system, smart home controls, and device management. But to view your Nest camera feeds, you need the Google Home app. It's not a dealbreaker, but switching between two apps to manage one system is a little annoying.

From what I've heard, ADT is working on combining everything into a single app. But for now, you're stuck toggling between the two.

The other minor annoyance is that you can't watch the Nest camera feeds on the command panel. There's a camera icon on the panel, but it only works with ADT's older proprietary cameras. It got grandfathered in. If you want to watch live video, you need to use the Google Home app on your phone or a Google Home Hub.

## Home automation and smart home features

<figure>
  <Image src={adtSmartLock} alt="ADT smart lock controlled from app" />
  <figcaption>
    The smart lock installed on my front door. I can lock and unlock it from the
    ADT app or set it to lock automatically when I arm the system.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

ADT supports Z-Wave compatible devices, so anything that uses Z-Wave (door locks, light switches, thermostats, garage door openers) can connect to the system and show up in the ADT app.

My setup is fairly simple on the automation side. I have a smart lock on the front door and a Nest thermostat. But if you're someone who wants to go all-in on the smart home experience, you can tie everything together under one system.

ADT has two types of automations:

**Scenes** are like one-tap presets. I have one set up so that when I arm the system, it also locks the front door and turns off the lights. One button press and it all happens at once.

**Automations** are more flexible. You set a trigger and an action with specific time frames. For example, you can have the system lock your door every morning at 5 AM, or turn the lights off Monday through Friday at 8 PM. It works with anything Z-Wave compatible, and the setup is pretty intuitive through the app.

## ADT pricing and contracts

Here's where things get a little more complicated. The pricing for the professionally installed system isn't listed on ADT's website the way the DIY system is, so it takes a phone call to get an actual quote.

For my setup (touchscreen panel, Nest doorbell camera, Nest outdoor camera, five door sensors, two glass break sensors, and two motion sensors), I'm paying **$48.99/month** and I'm on a **12-month agreement**. I paid around **$900 upfront** for all the equipment.

My receipt showed a $12/month discount from $60 down to $48. I'm honestly not sure if that was a genuine deal or just how they present the pricing, because I didn't see it advertised when I initially called in. Either way, if you call and ask for the same setup, I'd expect a similar price.

### How the contracts work now

ADT used to lock everyone into a 36-month contract. That's changed. Here's how it works now:

- **Pay for equipment upfront:** 12-month minimum agreement
- **Finance the equipment (no interest):** 36-month or 60-month agreement, with the equipment cost rolled into your monthly bill

I paid for everything upfront because I don't like long contracts. Twelve months is still more than I'd prefer, but I know I'll still want security a year from now, so it doesn't bother me much. It's a big improvement over the old three-year contracts.

### Is ADT worth it?

I think ADT is worth it if you're the type of person who wants a reliable, professionally installed system and doesn't want to deal with the DIY side of things. If you want good cameras, solid monitoring, and the peace of mind that comes with a company that's been doing this for over a century, it's a strong option.

The upfront cost is the biggest hurdle. Paying $900+ for equipment and then $48/month on top of that isn't cheap. But you're getting professional installation, a technician who knows where to place everything, and a system that works if someone breaks, they'll come fix it. You don't have to troubleshoot it yourself.

If you're really price-conscious and don't mind setting things up on your own, there are good DIY systems out there. ADT even has their own DIY version. But for the full experience with the touchscreen panel and professional install, this is what you're looking at.

## Who should get ADT?

I don't think ADT is for everybody. But here's who I'd recommend it to:

- **People who want things simple.** The system is easy to use. The panel is intuitive, the app works well, and the monitoring just runs in the background. You arm it when you leave, disarm it when you come home.
- **People who want a lot of cameras.** The Nest camera integration is excellent, and you can add cameras without your monthly price going up. If cameras are a priority, ADT makes that easy.
- **People who don't want to climb a ladder.** If you're like me and you'd rather not drill holes in your house and run wires for outdoor cameras, the professional install handles all of that.
- **People who want proven reliability.** Six monitoring stations, over 100 years in business, cellular backup. The infrastructure is there.

If you're someone who wants the cheapest possible option or you enjoy the DIY process, this might not be the best fit. But for the set-it-and-forget-it crowd who values reliability, ADT is hard to beat.
