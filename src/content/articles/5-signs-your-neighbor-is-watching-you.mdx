Most people are blessed enough to live next to a neighbor who is friendly or, at the very least, minds their own business and never gets in your way. Unfortunately, a few people have neighbors who aren't like that in the least — neighbors who cause issues and, yes, often spy on people.

If you're a bit suspicious that your neighbor is watching you, then read on. We've got five signs your neighbor is watching you, and we'll even tell you how you can handle the situation.

## 1. They're Always Around

Ever thought "my neighbor always comes outside when I do?" Well, that's the number one sign of a creepy neighbor. If your neighbor always seems to be appearing whenever you do, then that is a huge red flag — particularly if it happens over the course of multiple days or weeks.

Now, there is a good chance that your neighbor has exactly the same schedule as you, but let's be honest, the chances of that are low if it is happening a lot.

## 2. Window Watching

One of the big signs of a creepy neighbor is that they are always staring out of the window. You might find them lurking close to your window, or perhaps having a quick glance as they slowly walk past your home. In rare cases, you might even see them watching with a camera or phone just to spy on you — although, thankfully, this is incredibly rare.

If the neighbor notices that you have spotted them, they might quickly adjust to make it look like they weren't doing something creepy. But if you've noticed it a lot, then that neighbor is up to no good. This is probably one of the most unmistakable signs when it comes to how to know if someone is spying on you.

## 3. They Know Things About You They Shouldn't Really Know

Has your neighbor been commenting on your guests? Having discussions with you about conversations you've had that they should never have been able to hear? Do they know a lot about your movements that they really shouldn't? Well, your neighbor is probably spying on you. They might even want you to know that they are spying on you if they're dropping personal details into casual conversation that they really should not be able to know otherwise.

This is one of the worst types of spying a neighbor can do. It means that they are actively seeking out information about you — it crosses into the realm of stalking. If there is information they could have gained from rooting around in your garbage, it might even be a police matter. People have been driven out of their homes thanks to this level of spying.

## 4. Conversations About Your Routines

Sometimes you'll have conversations with your neighbor that will just creep you out. Conversations where it feels like they're trying to get as much information out of you as possible. They may try to find out about your routines, the names of your guests, where you work, and your working hours.

It will come across as a casual conversation, and your neighbor may be an expert at trying to get the information out of you. If you have always thought "my neighbor is always watching me" and you suddenly get one of these probing conversations, then shut it down immediately. No good can come of it.

## 5. You Notice Serious Signs of Snooping

Noticing footsteps around your home? Things appear to have been moved? Maybe you've noticed somebody has hidden something on your property such as a camera? That's a major sign you're being snooped on, and a massive red flag. If somebody has escalated the situation to where they are coming onto your property to gain information, then you've got serious problems that need to be fixed.

## What to Do If Your Neighbor is Watching You

Now that you have a bit of information on how to know if you're being watched, here are a few tips on how you can handle the situation:

- Log any incidents properly.
- Confront your neighbor and find out why they are doing it. Don't make the confrontation physical — just let them know that you know.
- If the problem is serious, tell the police. They might not do anything at the start unless the neighbor has broken the law, but it is always good to report. Especially report if you find hidden devices on your property.
- Add a bit more privacy around your home. You can add blinds or curtains to your windows, and privacy hedges and fences to your property.
- Use motion-sensitive lights. They will switch on if anybody tries to access your property at night and will help deter them.
