Moving is stressful enough without having to think about your security system. But if you're mid-contract with ADT and you just call to cancel service, you're looking at an early termination fee that can add up quickly. The good news is that ADT has a formal Movers Program specifically for this situation, and if you handle it correctly, you can transfer service to your new address without paying a dime in penalties.

This guide covers everything you need to know: when to call, what happens to your equipment, what to expect at the new address, and what to do if you're moving into a home that already has ADT installed. If that last situation applies to you, I'd start with [what to do when you buy a house with ADT](/home-security-systems/bought-a-house-with-adt) first.

## Should You Transfer or Cancel?

If you're mid-contract, transfer — don't cancel. Canceling ADT service before your contract ends triggers an early termination fee equal to 75% of the remaining monthly payments. On a $48.99/month plan with 8 months left, that's about $295 in fees for nothing.

ADT's Movers Program lets existing customers transfer their monitoring service to a new address without triggering that penalty. The contract terms transfer with you. You're not starting over — you're continuing the same agreement at a new location.

The only time canceling makes more sense than transferring is if you're moving somewhere that ADT doesn't service, moving internationally, or moving to a situation where security monitoring isn't practical (a temporary rental while you look for a permanent home, for example). Even then, call ADT first and explain the situation — there may be options.

## The Moving Checklist: What to Do and When

Getting the timing right makes this process a lot smoother.

**30 days before your move date:** Call ADT at <a href={`tel:${companyData.phoneUnformatted}`}>{companyData.phone}</a> and let them know you're moving. Tell them your new address and expected move date. ADT will schedule a technician visit at the new address and note the transfer in your account. The earlier you call, the easier it is to get a tech appointment that lines up with your actual move-in date.

**1-2 weeks before the move:** Confirm the installation appointment at the new address. Make sure someone will be home to let the technician in. If your plans have shifted, call ADT to adjust the date.

**The week of the move:** Do a final system test at the old address. Put your system in test mode (through the app or by calling ADT) and trip each sensor one last time to make sure everything is logging correctly. It's more of a peace-of-mind step than a required one, but it closes the loop.

**At the new address:** The ADT technician will walk the property, assess the entry points, and install sensors at all doors, windows, and any other spots they identify as vulnerable. They'll set up the panel, activate the system, give you a new PIN, and walk you through how everything works. The whole process typically takes 2 to 4 hours depending on the size of the home.

## What Happens to the Equipment?

This is the part that confuses most people. When you move, the sensors, panel, and most hardwired equipment stay in the old house. You don't take them with you, and you don't owe the new owner anything for leaving them behind.

ADT installs fresh equipment at the new address as part of the Movers Program. The cameras are a slightly different story — Nest cameras are not proprietary to ADT, so technically you own them and could take them. Call ADT to confirm the specifics for your situation, because whether the cameras transfer with you or stay can depend on whether they were included in a promotion or paid for separately.

<Admonition>
  Call ADT before uninstalling anything from the old home. Some equipment, especially hardwired sensors, should be left in place. Taking sensors with you won't save any money and could cause problems with your account transfer.
</Admonition>

The equipment at the new address is new. That's actually a benefit of the transfer process — you're not lugging old sensors to a new home and hoping they pair correctly. The tech brings everything needed for a clean installation.

## Moving into a Home With Existing ADT Equipment

If you're buying or renting a home that already has ADT equipment installed, you don't inherit the previous owner's contract. You'd start a fresh agreement with ADT. The equipment already in the home can lower or eliminate your upfront equipment cost, since the panel, sensors, and potentially cameras are already there.

Call ADT to have a technician assess what's installed. They'll determine which sensors are still functional, whether the panel is the current Command Panel model or an older system, and what (if anything) would need to be replaced or updated to bring the system up to current standards.

When choosing a plan at the new address, see my [breakdown of ADT monitoring plans for new homeowners](/home-security-systems/adt-monitoring-plans-for-new-homeowners) — it covers both tiers and helps you figure out whether you need the camera plan.

## Renters and ADT

ADT works in rentals. The sensors stick on with adhesive and don't require drilling, which makes them renter-friendly. The panel typically sits on a surface or mounts with minimal hardware.

Where things get more complicated is with hardwired outdoor cameras. Some installations require drilling into exterior walls or soffits. For those, you'd want to check with your landlord before scheduling the install. Most landlords are fine with a security system — some even appreciate that the property will have professional monitoring — but it's worth asking before the tech shows up.

## Using the Move as an Upgrade Opportunity

If your existing ADT system is a few years old, the move is a good time to ask about updating the equipment. The technician at the new address is starting fresh anyway, so this is a natural point to get the current Command Panel if you've been using an older push-button keypad, or to add Nest cameras if you haven't had them before.
