## Our Vivint rating

<CompanyRating company="vivint" />

We gave Vivint a 4.5 out of 5. The equipment and features are as good as anything on the market, and the app is genuinely excellent. The only reason it doesn't get a perfect score is pricing. You will pay more for Vivint than most competitors, and the cameras being proprietary to the service stings when you're already spending this much.

## The smart hub panel

Vivint's system runs through a touchscreen panel called the Smart Hub. If you've ever used an older alarm system with those tiny push buttons, this is a completely different experience. Changing a PIN code, bypassing a sensor, adjusting settings... it's all right there on the screen and pretty self-explanatory.

<Image
  src={panelSettings}
  alt="Adjusting settings on the Vivint smart hub touchscreen panel"
/>

From the panel you can arm and disarm the system, lock your doors, change the thermostat, and control anything else connected to the system. The monitoring team can also communicate with you through the panel during an emergency, which could be useful if you can't get to your phone during a break-in.

The panel connects to the monitoring center over cellular, so it works even when your Wi-Fi and power are down. There's a backup battery built in too.

That said, you're probably going to use the panel way less than you'd expect. I'd estimate 99% of what I do happens through the app on my phone.

### Shortcuts and automations

Vivint's app has a feature called shortcuts that lets you set up automations for the system. You pick a trigger (time of day, an event like arming the system or ringing the doorbell) and then choose what actions fire off when that trigger is met.

<Image
  src={shortcutsApp}
  alt="Setting up automation shortcuts in the Vivint app"
/>

A few examples of what you can do:

- Lock the front door and it automatically arms the alarm
- Arm the system after 9 PM and it turns off the lights, locks the doors, and adjusts the thermostat
- Someone rings the doorbell and specific lights turn on

I personally don't use this feature much, but if you're the type who wants to go all in on a true smart home setup, there's a lot to play with here.

The cameras are my favorite part of the system, and where I spend the most time in the app. I have the doorbell camera and the outdoor camera. I upgraded the outdoor camera with the floodlight add-on about a year after installation.

Both cameras produce really clear video. HD quality, solid night vision that picks up detail even in total darkness, and two-way talk so you can have a conversation with someone at the door from your phone or the panel. Audio records along with the video too.

The cameras connect wirelessly to the Smart Hub panel, which acts as your DVR. Even without Wi-Fi, the cameras will still record. You just won't be able to view footage remotely from your phone until the connection is back.

The doorbell camera is the exception since it can use your existing doorbell wiring. My original doorbell wasn't working when I bought the house, so the tech drilled a small hole and ran wiring to a nearby outlet. I tucked the cable behind the baseboard so you can't see it at all. ### Motion detection and recordings

Each camera has a built-in motion sensor. When activity is detected, you get a push notification and the camera starts recording. I had to tweak the detection zones and sensitivity settings when I first got the system because I was getting too many alerts. After some adjustments, it pretty much only triggers now when there's an actual person there.

<Image
  src={cameraZoneSettings}
  alt="Adjusting camera detection zone settings in the Vivint app"
/>

All recorded clips show up in the events tab of the app. You can watch them or download them to your phone. As new footage comes in, the oldest clips get auto-deleted. You'll get roughly 14 days of storage, though that fluctuates based on how much activity your cameras detect.

<Image
  src={appRecordings}
  alt="Viewing recorded camera clips in the Vivint app"
/>

There's also an option for full 24/7 continuous recording at $7 per month per camera. That bumps you up to 10 days of non-stop footage with a timeline view that flags where events occurred. It's a nice option to have, but at $7 per camera on top of what you're already paying, it adds up.

### The deter feature

When you activate deter mode on a camera and it detects motion, the lens lights up with a red ring and the camera plays a loud whistle. It's designed to let whoever is out there know they're being watched and recorded.

<Image
  src={deterNight}
  alt="Vivint outdoor camera deter mode activated at night with red ring"
/>

The outdoor camera with the floodlight takes this further. In deter mode, the floodlight kicks on, the whistle sounds, and the red ring lights up. All of that commotion grabs attention and makes the person look straight at the camera, giving you a clear shot of their face. It's actually a pretty clever approach to home security. Instead of just passively recording, the system is actively trying to prevent the incident from happening in the first place.

## Smart home devices

### Door lock

<Image src={smartLock} alt="Vivint smart lock installed on front door" />

The smart lock can be controlled from the app, the panel, or the keypad on the lock itself. Each person in the house gets their own PIN code, and you can change PINs remotely from the app. That's especially handy if you're renting your place out on Airbnb or having guests. Just swap the code between stays without being there.

If the battery dies, you can still use your regular key. The tech was also able to rekey the new Vivint lock to match my existing key, so I didn't end up carrying around an extra one.

I should be upfront about this, though. My door lock slowly stopped working around the three-year mark. I live in central Florida and my front door faces south. That door gets absolutely baked by the sun in the summer. I think the constant heat exposure wore the lock down over time. Warranty covered the replacement, but it was still annoying to deal with after spending this much on a system.

## Monitoring

Vivint's monitoring covers police, fire, and medical dispatch. Everything runs over a cellular connection, so you don't need a landline. If your power or Wi-Fi goes out, the system keeps working thanks to the cellular backup and the panel's built-in battery.

<Image
  src={armingSystem}
  alt="Arming the Vivint security system from the panel"
/>

I've never had a real break-in (thankfully), but I've accidentally set the alarm off a handful of times. The response was fast. Within seconds, the monitoring team was calling my phone. They can also communicate through the panel, which would be useful during an actual emergency.

One concern I had before getting the system was false alarms and police showing up for nothing. It's a non-issue. If you accidentally trip the alarm and disarm it within a few seconds, it cancels the dispatch. Sometimes the monitoring center doesn't even call because they can tell it was a false trigger. So if that's holding you back, don't let it.

## Customer service

I've seen a lot of complaints online about Vivint's customer service. My experience has been mostly fine. Every time I've called, I've gotten a US-based agent and had my issue handled without a ton of back and forth. No billing problems either.

One tip: skip the website chat. They outsource it overseas and I've never once had a useful interaction through it. Just call directly. You'll save yourself the headache.

## Vivint pricing breakdown

Here's how the costs break down:

**Monthly monitoring plans:**

- $29/month for basic monitoring (police, fire, medical, plus the app)
- $39/month adds smart home features on top of monitoring
- +$5/month for each camera you add to your plan

My setup includes smart home features plus two cameras, which puts me at $49 per month.

**Equipment costs:**

You have two ways to pay for equipment: upfront or financed.

I paid roughly $1,200 to $1,300 upfront for everything. That's a big check to write at once, but it means my service is month to month. No contract. I can cancel whenever I want.

If you finance, Vivint offers 0% APR over 42 or 60 months. They roll the equipment cost into your monthly bill so you're paying one amount. Here's the catch, though. Financing ties you to a service agreement for the full length of the financing term. You can't cancel monitoring while you still owe on the equipment. To get out, you'd have to pay off the remaining equipment balance first.

If you can swing the upfront cost, do it. The flexibility of month-to-month service is worth the bigger initial payment.

## Who should get Vivint

Vivint makes the most sense for homeowners who want professional installation, reliable 24/7 monitoring, a polished app experience, and real smart home integration all from one provider. If you want everything under one roof and don't mind paying a premium for it, this is the system.

It's also a good fit if you live in a larger home. The professional tech will assess your property, identify vulnerable entry points, and customize sensor placement. That's harder to do yourself with a DIY kit.

## Who should skip Vivint

If you only want security cameras and don't care about a full alarm system, you're overpaying with Vivint. A Ring or Nest camera setup would cover you for less money and without any monthly monitoring commitment.

If budget is tight, $49 per month or more (plus the upfront equipment cost) is a real financial commitment. Something like SimpliSafe or Ring Alarm would give you solid protection at a lower price point.

And if the idea of a multi-year contract makes you uncomfortable and you can't afford to pay upfront, that's another reason to look elsewhere. Getting locked into a 42 or 60 month financing agreement is a long time.

## What I'd do differently

If I were starting over, I'd still pick Vivint. But I'd adjust a few things.

I'd start with fewer cameras and add more later once I understood exactly where I needed coverage. It's tempting to go all in during installation, but cameras are the most expensive part of the package and it's smarter to be strategic about placement.

The door lock situation also taught me something. If your front door gets a lot of direct sun, mention it to the tech during installation. There might be a more durable option or a different placement that holds up better to heat exposure.

## Is Vivint worth it?

For me, yes. We use this system every single day. The app is the best I've used in the home security space, the cameras give me real peace of mind when we're traveling, and having everything in one place just makes daily life easier. Three years in and I'm still happy with the decision.

But I get that it's not for everyone. The monthly cost is higher than most competitors. The equipment is expensive whether you pay upfront or finance. And the cameras being locked to Vivint's ecosystem is frustrating when you're already spending this much money.

Where Vivint separates itself is the total package. Professional installation so you don't have to mess with it. Monitoring that actually responds quickly. An app that consolidates cameras, locks, thermostat, and the alarm into one place. Smart home automations that tie everything together. That combination is genuinely hard to find elsewhere.

If you want that level of integration and you're willing to pay for it, Vivint delivers. If price is the priority, there are solid alternatives that will protect your home for less.

## Vivint FAQ

### How much does Vivint cost per month?

Monthly plans range from $29 to $49 depending on your features. Basic monitoring (police, fire, medical plus the app) starts at $29. Adding smart home features bumps it to $39. Each camera is an additional $5 per month.

### Does Vivint require a contract?

Not if you pay for the equipment upfront. That puts you on month-to-month service with no commitment. If you finance the equipment, you're locked into a service agreement for either 42 or 60 months depending on the financing term.

### Can Vivint use my existing sensors?

Yes. When the tech comes out, they'll check your existing hardwired sensors. If they're still working, Vivint can take them over. Any that are dead get swapped with Vivint's wireless sensors.

### Do Vivint cameras work without Wi-Fi?

The cameras will still record locally through the panel even without Wi-Fi. But you won't be able to view live feeds or pull up recordings from the app until your internet connection is back.

### Can I use Vivint cameras without the monitoring plan?

Technically the cameras still record to the panel locally. But without the monitoring plan, you lose access to the app, remote viewing, cloud storage, and all the smart features. At that point, the cameras become pretty limited.

### Does Vivint work with Alexa and Google Assistant?

Yes, Vivint works with both Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant. You can use voice commands to arm the system, control smart home devices, and view camera feeds on compatible smart displays.

### Is Vivint better than ADT?

They're in the same tier. ADT has been around longer and offers more monitoring options (landline and cellular). Vivint wins on smart home features, app quality, and camera technology. It really comes down to what you prioritize.
